Our 5 Core Manifestos

The Manifesto.

Jhalmuri Janta Party: “Online democratic movements need flavour too.”

  1. 01

    No Serious Criminal Background in Politics: JJP believes that individuals facing serious criminal charges related to corruption, violence, organized crime, or abuse of public office should not be given election tickets until cleared by the judiciary. Public service should be built on trust, accountability, and integrity — not fear or influence.

  2. 02

    Expertise-Based Ministry Allocation: JJP believes ministries should be led with competence, domain understanding, and expert guidance. Education, experience, and subject expertise must play a major role while assigning ministries, ensuring that critical national sectors are handled by individuals with relevant knowledge and capable advisory ecosystems.

  3. 03

    End Unnecessary VIP & VVIP Culture: JJP supports only the minimum necessary security protocols required for safety purposes. Public representatives are elected to serve citizens — not to remain disconnected behind excessive convoys, blocked roads, unnecessary privileges, and symbolic displays of power funded by taxpayers.

  4. 04

    National Transparency & Accountability Portal: JJP proposes a public digital transparency portal where every government project, scheme, announcement, and expenditure can be tracked by citizens in real time. The portal will include project progress tracking, timeline updates, public expenditure visibility, complaint filing systems, and mandatory government responses within 24–72 hours. Governance should not happen behind closed doors. Citizens deserve visibility.

  5. 05

    Performance-Based Accountability System: JJP believes accountability must have consequences. Government bodies repeatedly failing transparency standards, ignoring public grievances, or crossing fixed accountability thresholds should face public review mechanisms, institutional penalties, and democratic re-evaluation processes. Power without accountability weakens democracy.
